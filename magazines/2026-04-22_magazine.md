## SQUAD-WIDE PATTERN ANALYSIS

### [Constraint] — Systemic Neuromuscular Suppression

Every athlete in the assessed cohort presents with **mRSI below the Smallest Worthwhile Change (SWC) threshold**. The modified Reactive Strength Index is the single most sensitive indicator of neuromuscular fatigue (Gathercole et al.), carrying a weight of 3 in the N1 Load-Probability model. A squad-wide mRSI suppression of this nature is not a coincidence of individual variation — it is a collective physiological signal.

Jump height (all below SWC), takeoff velocity (all below SWC), and flight time (majority below SWC) compound the picture. The squad's neuromuscular output has degraded across every primary expression metric.

### [Lens] — Mechanical Compensation Is Active Squad-Wide

The N1 V10 Biomechanical Forensics Engine identifies compensation patterns from force-displacement loop signatures. Across the six assessed athletes:

- **Extended Brake Phase** detected in 4 of 6 athletes — the countermovement is slower and less forceful than individual baseline; athletes are spending excess time in the eccentric phase without generating correspondingly greater peak eccentric force.
- **P1 Phase Dominance** detected in 3 of 6 athletes — early propulsive phase is disproportionately elevated against a suppressed net propulsive impulse; a hallmark of front-loading effort to compensate for a late-phase force production deficit.
- **Over-Brake Pattern** detected in 1 athlete — excess energy absorbed in the braking phase is not being recycled into propulsive output; stretch-shortening cycle transfer efficiency is compromised.
- **Critical Braking Asymmetry (>15% threshold)** detected in 1 athlete — one limb is absorbing disproportionate braking load; highest acute injury risk in the cohort.
- **Asymmetry at clinical monitoring threshold (10–15%)** detected in 2 additional athletes — emerging unilateral load redistribution under fatigue.

The co-occurrence of systemic output suppression and active mechanical compensation in every assessed athlete defines the CRITICAL classification under the Harmonizer matrix: *fatigued AND mechanically compensating — the highest-risk combination for acute injury under continued load.*

### [Output] — Immediate Operational Directive

**No high-intensity training for any assessed athlete.** Recovery modalities take priority. Technical corrections are contraindicated under this level of systemic fatigue — entraining altered mechanics during a fatigued state reinforces compensatory movement patterns.

Session design for this cohort, if any contact is unavoidable:
- Low CNS demand only
- No plyometric or reactive work
- No repeated sprint or jump loading
- Soft tissue and recovery modalities prioritised

- Prioritise single-pattern corrections (address dominant compensation pattern per athlete)
- Progressive bilateral jump reintroduction with force plate monitoring
- Do not stack volume and intensity simultaneously on re-entry

## METHODOLOGY

**Assessment Tool:** Hawkin Dynamics Force Plate — Countermovement Jump (CMJ)
**Classification Framework:** N1 V10 Three-Engine Pipeline

**Engine 1 — N1 V10 Biomechanical Forensics**
Detects mechanical compensation from force-displacement loop signatures. Patterns evaluated: P1 Phase Dominance, Extended Brake Phase, Over-Brake Pattern, Bilateral Asymmetry, and SSC Efficiency Loss. Output: STABLE | COMPENSATING (Minor / Moderate / Severe).

**Engine 2 — Load-Probability Engine**
Zone7-style systemic burnout probability derived from weighted SWC flag accumulation. Key weights: mRSI (×3), Jump Height (×2), Takeoff Velocity (×2), Braking RFD (×2). Maximum score: 14. Output: LOW RISK | MODERATE RISK | HIGH RISK.

**Engine 3 — The Harmonizer**
Decision matrix combining Engine 1 and Engine 2 outputs into a single operational classification:

| N1 V10 | Load-Probability | Classification |
|---|---|---|
| STABLE | LOW RISK | READY |
| STABLE | MODERATE / HIGH RISK | MAINTENANCE REQUIRED |
| COMPENSATING | LOW RISK | TECHNICAL INTERVENTION |
| COMPENSATING | MODERATE / HIGH RISK | CRITICAL: MANDATORY REST |

**SWC Method:** Hopkins et al. — Smallest Worthwhile Change relative to individual athlete baseline. All directional flags (▲ Above SWC / ▼ Below SWC) are relative to each athlete's own reference score, not population norms.
