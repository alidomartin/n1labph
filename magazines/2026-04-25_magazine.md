## METHODOLOGY

**Assessment Tool:** Hawkin Dynamics Force Plate — Countermovement Jump (CMJ)
**Classification Framework:** N1 V10 Three-Engine Pipeline

**Engine 1 — N1 V10 Biomechanical Forensics**
Detects mechanical compensation from force-displacement loop signatures. Patterns evaluated: P1 Phase Dominance, Extended Brake Phase, Over-Brake Pattern, Bilateral Asymmetry, and SSC Efficiency Loss. Output: STABLE | COMPENSATING (Minor / Moderate / Severe).

**Engine 2 — Load-Probability Engine**
Zone7-style systemic burnout probability derived from weighted SWC flag accumulation. Key weights: mRSI (×3), Jump Height (×2), Takeoff Velocity (×2), Braking RFD (×2). Maximum score: 14. Output: LOW RISK | MODERATE RISK | HIGH RISK.

**Engine 3 — The Harmonizer**
Decision matrix combining Engine 1 and Engine 2 outputs into a single operational classification:

| N1 V10 | Load-Probability | Classification |
|---|---|---|
| STABLE | LOW RISK | READY |
| STABLE | MODERATE / HIGH RISK | MAINTENANCE REQUIRED |
| COMPENSATING | LOW RISK | TECHNICAL INTERVENTION |
| COMPENSATING | MODERATE / HIGH RISK | CRITICAL: MANDATORY REST |

**SWC Method:** Hopkins et al. — Smallest Worthwhile Change relative to individual athlete baseline. All directional flags (▲ Above SWC / ▼ Below SWC) are relative to each athlete's own reference score, not population norms.
