## SQUAD-WIDE PATTERN ANALYSIS

### [Constraint] — Systemic Neuromuscular Suppression: Status Unresolved

Every athlete in the assessed cohort continues to present with **mRSI below the Smallest Worthwhile Change (SWC) threshold**. The modified Reactive Strength Index is the single most sensitive indicator of neuromuscular fatigue (Gathercole et al.), carrying a weight of 3 in the N1 Load-Probability model. The squad-wide mRSI range of 0.60 – 0.78 is unchanged from the prior session, as no new recovery data has been loaded.

Jump height (all below SWC), takeoff velocity (all below SWC), and flight time (majority below SWC) remain suppressed across the full cohort. The neuromuscular output profile is uniformly degraded with no confirmed recovery trajectory.

### [Lens] — Mechanical Compensation Patterns Persist Squad-Wide

The N1 V10 Biomechanical Forensics Engine identifies compensation patterns from force-displacement loop signatures. Across the six assessed athletes:

- **Extended Brake Phase** detected in 5 of 6 athletes — the countermovement is slower and less forceful than individual baseline; athletes are spending excess time in the eccentric phase without generating correspondingly greater peak eccentric force.
- **P1 Phase Dominance** detected in 3 of 6 athletes — early propulsive phase is disproportionately elevated against a suppressed net propulsive impulse; a hallmark of front-loading effort to compensate for a late-phase force production deficit.
- **Over-Brake Pattern** detected in 1 athlete — excess energy absorbed in the braking phase is not being recycled into propulsive output; stretch-shortening cycle transfer efficiency is compromised.
- **Critical Braking Asymmetry (>15% threshold)** detected in 1 athlete — one limb is absorbing disproportionate braking load; highest acute injury risk in the cohort.
- **Critical Propulsive Asymmetry (>15% threshold)** detected in 1 athlete — the concentric phase is being driven asymmetrically, with disproportionate unilateral force contribution.
- **Asymmetry at clinical monitoring threshold (10–15%)** detected in 2 additional athletes in the braking phase, and 1 athlete in the landing phase.

The co-occurrence of systemic output suppression and active mechanical compensation in every assessed athlete maintains the CRITICAL classification under the Harmonizer matrix: *fatigued AND mechanically compensating — the highest-risk combination for acute injury under continued load.*

## METHODOLOGY

**Assessment Tool:** Hawkin Dynamics Force Plate — Countermovement Jump (CMJ)
**Classification Framework:** N1 V10 Three-Engine Pipeline

**Engine 1 — N1 V10 Biomechanical Forensics**
Detects mechanical compensation from force-displacement loop signatures. Patterns evaluated: P1 Phase Dominance, Extended Brake Phase, Over-Brake Pattern, Bilateral Asymmetry, and SSC Efficiency Loss. Output: STABLE | COMPENSATING (Minor / Moderate / Severe).

**Engine 2 — Load-Probability Engine**
Zone7-style systemic burnout probability derived from weighted SWC flag accumulation. Key weights: mRSI (×3), Jump Height (×2), Takeoff Velocity (×2), Braking RFD (×2). Maximum score: 14. Output: LOW RISK | MODERATE RISK | HIGH RISK.

**Engine 3 — The Harmonizer**
Decision matrix combining Engine 1 and Engine 2 outputs into a single operational classification:

| N1 V10 | Load-Probability | Classification |
|---|---|---|
| STABLE | LOW RISK | READY |
| STABLE | MODERATE / HIGH RISK | MAINTENANCE REQUIRED |
| COMPENSATING | LOW RISK | TECHNICAL INTERVENTION |
| COMPENSATING | MODERATE / HIGH RISK | CRITICAL: MANDATORY REST |

**SWC Method:** Hopkins et al. — Smallest Worthwhile Change relative to individual athlete baseline. All directional flags (▲ Above SWC / ▼ Below SWC) are relative to each athlete's own reference score, not population norms.
